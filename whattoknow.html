What to Know

There's endless resources out there for getting started drawing digitally. From YouTube to tutorials to lifestyle blogs, if you ever have a question about a specific effect or tool, all you need to do is Google it. Here's a few things to get you started.

Devices

I use an iPad for my drawing, but that is by no means a required purchase. I like my iPad because it provides a larger surface area and the ability to use a stylus. Plus, there are a lot of digital drawing apps for iPads, since they are used by a lot of designers. But you could also use your phone to start. The screen will be smaller, but your phone will also be compatible with a lot of the drawing apps. Your laptop or desktop computer is also an option. Chances are, the free apps you're looking for may not be available on an actual computer as they're meant for mobile devices, but there are a lot of software options out there, if you're looking to use what you already have and not purchase another device.

Apps

There are a lot of applications available for drawing digitally, and it all comes down to artist preference. A couple free ones that I love are Adobe Illustrator Draw and Adobe Fresco. Draw is a more basic version of Adobe Illustrator, while Fresco has more textures, brushes, and effects. The apps themselves are free but so require an Adobe plan and account.
